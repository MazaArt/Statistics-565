\section*{Introduction}
This report summarizes the completed replication and extension project for \textit{Topological Data Analysis of Financial Time Series: Landscapes of Crashes} by Marian Gidea and Yuri Katz (\url{https://arxiv.org/pdf/1703.04385}). Following my proposal and intermediate report, the main objective is to reproduce the empirical pipeline for topological indicators based on persistence landscapes and to assess whether induced time series behave in an informative way prior to major U.S.\ equity stress episodes, benchmarked against standard volatility measures. In complementary terms, the question is whether multivariate geometry of recent returns carries incremental information relative to dispersion and dependence summaries alone.

The work has two components. First, I replicate the paper's baseline U.S.\ implementation: Yahoo Finance inputs, rolling multivariate clouds, persistence landscapes (\(w\in\{50,100\}\)), and anchored diagnostics around the dot-com and Lehman dates. Second, I apply identical code to an extended horizon through 2020, including the COVID disruption, so that conclusions can be compared across an episode outside the authors' estimation window without changing the substantive procedure midway. Subsequent sections summarize the underlying construction, replication outputs, extension results, and limitations.

\section*{Background and motivation}
At each date \(t\), the construction fixes a backward-looking window of length \(w\) over \(d\) traded indices (here \(d=4\)). Daily log-returns yield vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^d\), so the trailing \(w\) observations form a point cloud \(X_t\subset\mathbb{R}^d\) describing simultaneous index behavior rather than unrelated univariate traces. Means, variances, and correlations characterize much of \(X_t\) through first- and second-order structure; the motivating claim in Gidea and Katz is that regimes prior to crises may reorganize geometric features visible only when the full cloud is summarized at multiple spatial scales---for example redistribution of density or stable loop-like structure across resolutions---prior to unmistakable shocks in volatility on every coordinate. Persistent homology operationalizes qualitative ``shape'' in a repeatable fashion.

Roughly speaking, persistent homology analyzes connectivity of thickened neighborhoods (\(\epsilon\)-neighborhood complexes) built from \(X_t\) as \(\epsilon\) increases from zero. Focusing on homology in degree \(H_1\) tracks topological loops: each surviving loop admits a lifetime between birth \(b\) and death \(d\), yielding a persistence diagram \(D^{(1)}_t\) of \(\{(b,d)\}\)-pairs summarized by persistence landscapes \(\lambda(\cdot)\); landscape norms \(\|\lambda\|_1\) and \(\|\lambda\|_2\) reduce this information to integrable scalars that update as \(t\) advances. The replicated implementation follows the authors' \(H_1\) / Vietoris--Rips emphasis and produces norms aligned alongside VIX rather than interpreting coordinates margin by margin alone.

Within that framework their intent is statistical early warning: systematically trending topological mass before anchored crash dates would corroborate multivariate deformation that classical aggregates underweight. Economic intuition is suggestive rather than strict: systemic stories of tightened linkages coincide qualitatively with geometric shifts in \(X_t\). Because crisis mechanisms vary and histories are scarce, reproducibility exercises and disciplined out-of-sample extension remain necessary to separate robust behavior from illustrative windows; that is precisely what replication and COVID extrapolation furnish here.

\section*{Replication: Setup and Main Findings}
\subsection*{Data and pipeline}
The data source is Yahoo Finance (\texttt{yfinance}), corresponding to my replication notebook. Daily adjusted closes are retrieved for:
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
    \item S\&P 500 (\texttt{\^{}GSPC})
    \item DJIA (\texttt{\^{}DJI})
    \item NASDAQ Composite (\texttt{\^{}IXIC})
    \item Russell 2000 (\texttt{\^{}RUT})
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}
VIX (\texttt{\^{}VIX}) enters as an external benchmark mirrored against landscape norms throughout, consistent with the paper's juxtaposition against implied volatility. Computational steps coincide with their published sequencing:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Construct daily log-returns \(r_{i,j}=\log(P_{i,j}/P_{i-1,j})\).
    \item Compile rolling \(w\)-day clouds \(X_t\subset\mathbb{R}^4\) for \(w\in\{50,100\}\).
    \item Compute \(H_1\) persistent homology with a Vietoris--Rips filtration using \texttt{ripser}.
    \item Estimate persistence landscapes and record \(L^1\), \(L^2\) norms at each trailing window end-date.
    \item Execute pre-anchor diagnostics (variance spectra, rolling variance, Kendall-\(\tau\) trend tests aligned with interim course materials).
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Extension: Post-Paper Sample and COVID-Era Test}
\subsection*{Extension design}
The same codebase is rerun with only an extended Yahoo Finance pull through 2020 so that comparisons for the COVID episode do not hinge on discretionary changes to filtration or homology code introduced between samples.

Exported outputs mirror the replication bundle:
\begin{itemize}
    \item persistence landscape norm series (\(w=50\), \(w=100\)),
    \item aligned 4-index returns,
    \item VIX benchmark.

\subsection*{COVID-era stress test}
Because COVID lies outside Gidea and Katz's published horizon, Figure~\ref{fig:ext_covid} constitutes an out-of-sample check. Ahead of the February--March 2020 selloff, normalized \(L^1\) remains near the bottom of the plotted interval while VIX accelerates sharply, so this specification (\(w=50\); four-equity universe) affords limited justification for interpreting \(L^1\) as an early-warning screen for COVID-specific stress.

Such a divergence is plausible when the shock transmits quickly through repricing tied to unfolding news rather than through the slower evolution of intra-window geometry summarized by homology on a fifty-day horizon. Robustness analyses (alternate window lengths, larger asset sets, diagnostics aimed at abrupt breaks) lie outside the present scope; nevertheless the pattern counsels restraint in generalizing unqualified early-warning claims beyond the historical episodes originally documented.

\section*{Conclusion}
Replication demonstrates that persistence landscape norms can register familiar stress eras; congruence among figures and Kendall summaries is strongest for Lehman among the anchored cases reproduced here. Dot-com illustrates dispersion across diagnostics, implying that extrapolating one template to every episode would overstate uniformity.

Together with Figure~\ref{fig:ext_covid}, the extension indicates that analogous early-warning conclusions need not survive every newer shock absent retuning or complementary signals. Taken as a package, evidence supports interpreting topological trackers as exploratory complements to volatility benchmarks, calibrated case by case rather than asserted as universally leading predictors pending further empirical work.
